Adobo

Description

What is adobo?

Adobo is way of preserving food by ancient filipino natives in pre-refregiration days, Adobo utilizes the acid in the vinegar and the high salt content of soy sauce to produce an undesirable environment for bacteria.

The term adobo is derived from the Spanish word "adobar", meaning marinade. The practice of marinating meat in a flavorful mixture made from vinegar, salt, garlic, paprika, and oregano was common to Spanish cooking. Spanish colonists gave the name “adobo” to the cooking method indigenous to the Philippines, as their marinades were so similar.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs your meat choice(Chicken or Pork)
  • 2 tablespoons of Vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic (chopped)
  • 1 onion(minced)
  • 1/3 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup of white vinegar
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground pepper
  • 1 cup hot water or more as needed
  • 1/4 cup of oystersauce
  • 1/2 tablespoon sugar

Instructions

  1. If you have Pork cut it into cubes equally, for Chicken you can cut it in reasonable proportions so it will cook evenly
  2. Heat oil in a cooking pan at medium-low heat
  3. When the oil is hot enough, Saute the minced onion first(?) then add garlic
  4. When the garlic is brown add the meat
  5. Saute the meat until its slightly brown
  6. Add pepper,soysauce and oystersauce
  7. Saute it until the meat absorb the flavor
  8. Add water
  9. Add bayleaf
  10. Cover it with Lid, and simmer it in low heat
  11. When the meat is tender(to your liking), Add vinegar but don't stir it(?)
  12. Cover it with lid and simmer it again
  13. When the water is reduced you can stir it

    14. (optional): You can fry the meat in small amount of oil or sauce in other pan to enhance its flavor

  14. Serve it. Enjoy!!
